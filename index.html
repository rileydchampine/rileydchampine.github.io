Riley D. Champine

Cartographer, GIS analyst, graphic designer, bassist, soccer referee, cyclist, etc.

I recently graduated from the University of Oregon with a B.S. in Geography and a minor in Planning, Public Policy & Management. My academic focuses were Geographic Information Science, cartography, and active transportation advocacy. Right now, I work in Silicon Valley as a GIS Tech for a big tech company.

I used to be a part of the UO InfoGraphics Lab where I created maps and graphics for an atlas about ungulates in Wyoming and also helped maintain a geodatabase of over 80 buildings and 20,000 rooms on campus.

Outside of work, I am most likely to be found on a soccer field (refereeing not playing), stubbornly fighting through Bay Area traffic by bike, or playing bass guitar.
